Notes, references & data handling

No PHI. Only initials, age, sex, date/time and a free-text comment are stored. Do not enter names, MRNs or DOBs. Data is saved locally in this browser only and never leaves the device; use Export for a portable JSON backup.

CQS — Catatonia Quick Screen (Luccarelli et al., 2025): any one of excitement, mutism, staring, or posturing = positive screen → proceed to full rating.
BFCRS — Bush–Francis Catatonia Rating Scale (Bush et al., 1996), 23 items, 0–3 (six items 0/3 only). Screening instrument = first 14 items; ≥2 present = positive screen.
PCRS — Pediatric Catatonia Rating Scale (Benarous et al., 2016), 20 items, max 60, suggestive threshold ≥9. The item set here approximates the published scale (BFCRS anchors for shared items, plus the documented pediatric additions). Verify exact items/anchors against the source before clinical use.

A decision-support aid, not a diagnostic device. Clinical judgement governs.

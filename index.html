References, attribution & data handling

No PHI. Only initials, age, sex, date/time and a free-text comment are stored. Do not enter names, MRNs or DOBs. Data is saved locally in this browser only and never leaves the device; use Export for a portable JSON backup.

Scales & sources
CQS — Catatonia Quick Screen. Luccarelli J, et al. Acta Psychiatrica Scandinavica (2025). Any one of excitement, mutism, staring, or posturing = positive screen → proceed to a full rating.
BFCRS — Bush–Francis Catatonia Rating Scale. Bush G, Fink M, Petrides G, Dowling F, Francis A. Catatonia. I. Rating scale and standardized examination. Acta Psychiatr Scand. 1996;93(2):129–136. 23 items scored 0–3 (six items 0/3 only); screening instrument = first 14 items, ≥2 present = positive screen. Official training materials: University of Rochester (Oldham, Wortzel & Francis).
  Official resources ↗ bfcrs.urmc.edu · interactive calculator · item scoring videos · training modules · video playlist
PCRS — Pediatric Catatonia Rating Scale. Benarous X, Consoli A, Raffin M, et al. Validation of the Pediatric Catatonia Rating Scale (PCRS). Schizophr Res. 2016;176(2–3):378–386. 20 items, max 60, suggestive threshold ≥9.
